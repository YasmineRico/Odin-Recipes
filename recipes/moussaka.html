Traditional Moussaka Recipe

Description

Moussaka is a traditional Greek dish that is like an eggplant casserole. The recipe calls for eggplant, potatoes, beef or lamb, finishing with bechamel sauce. It is the ultimate comfort food.

Moussaka is a core memory for me. While living in Belgium I would frequent Fin de Siecle, highly recommend eating there. I took my first bite and was hooked. Now I make it at home for special occasions, even my 2 year old God-Daughter enjoys it!

Ingredients

  • 6 Eggplants
  • 5 Potatoes
  • Vegetable oil
  • Ground Beef or Lamb 750g
  • 2 Red Onions
  • 1 Tin Chopped Tomatoes
  • 2 Tbsp Tomato Paste
  • 1 Teaspoon Sugar
  • 1 Glass of Red Wine
  • Pinch of Salt and Pepper
  • 1 Bay Leaf
  • 1/4 Cup of Olive Oil

Bechamel Sauce Ingredients

  • 900ml Milk
  • 120g Butter
  • 120g Flour
  • 2 Egg Yolks
  • 100g Parmiagiano-Reggiano or Kefalotyri

Steps to Cook

  1. Prepare the eggplants by removing the stalks and cut into thick slices. Season with Salt and set in colander for 30 minutes.
  2. AFter 30 minutes rinse the eggplants and squeeze with your hands to get rid of excess water. Place in baking dish when dry.
  3. While the eggplants are baking, slice potatoes by 1/4 of an inch. Place the eggplants in the bottom of the baking, add potatoes. bake for 20 minutes.
  4. Heat a large pan to medium-high add olive oil. Stir in Chopped onions saute until golden. Stir in the ground meat once it has browned add garlic and tomato paste. Pour the glass of Red Wine. Add tomatoes, sugar, bay leaf and salt/pepper to taste.
  5. Bring to a boil then turn the heat down and simmer with lid on for 30 minutes or until most juices have evaporated.
  6. For the Bechamel sauce we will use a large pan and melt butter over low-medium heat. Whisk in the flour to make a paste. Steadily add in warmed milk while whisking the mixture. This will ensure there are no lumps in the sauce. Once complete the sauce should have the consistency of thick cream.
  7. Once the Bechamel sauce is finished remove from the heat. We will now add in the egg yolks, salt, pepper, pinch of nutmeg and grated cheese. Make sure to leave enough cheese to sprinkle on top of the Moussaka for later. Whisk the mixture together add salt to taste.
  8. Now it's time for assembly! In a large baking dish, butter the bottom and sides and layer the potatoes first. Next we will add half of the eggplants and pur in all of the meat sauce, spreading it evenly. Add the second layer of eggplants and top it off with the bechamel sauce smoothing over with a spatula. Sprinkle the remaining cheese and top.
  9. Preheat oven to 350F/180C. Bake the moussaka for 60 minutes or until golden brown. Leave it out to cool down.
  10. Now you're ready to feast on delicious homemade moussaka! As an added tip its best to enjoy with a glass of wine. Enjoy!
